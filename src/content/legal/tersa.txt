 3. How Tersa Works
 Tersa does not connect to bank, card, brokerage, payroll, tax, or accounting accounts. Users enter
 records manually or import supported files, review staged information, and confirm changes. Tersa does
 not initiate payments, transfers, trades, tax filings, or credit applications.
 The local encrypted database is Tersa's working source of truth. Optional CloudKit sync projects
 confirmed records and encrypted attachments into the user's Apple iCloud environment. The Developer
 does not operate a separate Tersa account or general-purpose financial-data server.

 Tersa's deterministic financial engine calculates postings, balances, schedules, budgets, forecasts, and
 reports. The bundled local AI may propose drafts, but it cannot autonomously commit, delete, export, or
 sync canonical financial records. The user must review and confirm consequential changes.

 6. Local Storage and Encryption
 Tersa stores the workspace in a SQLCipher-encrypted database in the app's protected container. The
 database key is randomly generated and stored in the iOS Keychain with device-only, unlocked-device
 protection. The database and attachments also use iOS file protection.
 Attachments are encrypted separately with authenticated encryption. Tersa uses separate key lifecycles
 for the database, attachments, portable backups, and household sharing. This separation reduces risk
 but does not make compromise impossible.

 If the database key is unavailable, Tersa is designed to fail closed rather than silently create an
 unencrypted replacement. Protecting the iPhone, passcode, Apple Account, and backup password
 remains the user's responsibility.

 7. Local AI and Receipt Recognition
 The bundled language model runs on the iPhone. Tersa does not configure it to call an external AI
 service, browse the internet, read arbitrary files, query the full database, or mutate records directly. It
 receives only context selected for the task and returns an untrusted proposal that is checked by
 deterministic code.
 Receipt capture and optical character recognition run through Apple on-device frameworks. The local
 model receives bounded recognized text, not receipt pixels. A user can reject or edit every proposed
 transaction before it becomes a financial record. An optional receipt image or PDF is stored only if the
 user chooses to attach it.

 AI and OCR output may be wrong, incomplete, duplicated, misleading, or offensive. Prompts, proposals,
 and learned mappings are kept locally and can be cleared separately. Only user-confirmed canonical
 records are eligible for CloudKit sync.

 8. Apple CloudKit and Individual Sync
 If the user enables individual sync, Tersa uploads confirmed workspace projections and eligible
 attachments to a private CloudKit database associated with the user's Apple Account. Apple provides
 account authentication, storage, transport, and synchronization under Apple's terms and privacy policy.

 Individual CloudKit records use Apple platform protections and Tersa does not operate a separate sync
 server. Tersa does not describe individual CloudKit records as having the additional household
 content-encryption layer unless the app expressly says so.
 CloudKit availability, retention, recovery, quota, and account access depend on Apple systems and the
 user's Apple Account. Disabling sync stops future synchronization but may not immediately erase records
 already retained by Apple or another device.

 9. Household Sharing and End-to-End Encryption
 A household owner may create a shared CloudKit zone and invite participants. Tersa encrypts household
 financial payloads and attachments on a participant device before CloudKit upload. Each authorized
 device receives wrapped workspace-key material. The Developer has no master key designed to decrypt
 household content.

 End-to-end encryption does not prevent invited participants from seeing the shared workspace.
 Participants may add, edit, reconcile, or delete shared records according to the current role and
 subscription state. A participant may also view information on a compromised device, capture
 screenshots, or export records outside Tersa.
 Apple necessarily processes CloudKit account, sharing, routing, device, and service metadata and stores
 opaque encrypted payloads. Tersa minimizes application-level routing metadata but does not control
 Apple's independent processing.

 The owner should invite only trusted people, remove participants when access is no longer appropriate,
 and understand that a removed participant may retain prior exports, screenshots, backups, or information
 already learned.

 10. Exchange Rates and Network Requests
 Tersa permits network access only for narrowly defined services: Apple StoreKit and App Store functions,
 Apple CloudKit and sharing, the configured exchange-rate provider, and user-initiated external legal
 pages.

 For an exchange-rate lookup, Tersa sends only the base currency, quote currency, and requested date to
 api.frankfurter.dev using an ephemeral HTTPS session. The request is designed without cookies,
 credentials, or a body. The provider and network intermediaries may still receive ordinary connection data
 such as IP address, time, and device-network information under their own practices.

 Rates may be delayed, unavailable, rounded, revised, or inaccurate and are provided for record-keeping
 estimates. A user should verify a rate against an authoritative source before making a payment, filing tax,
 valuing an asset, or taking another consequential action.
