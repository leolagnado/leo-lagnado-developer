3. How Tersa Works
Tersa does not connect to bank, card, brokerage, payroll, tax, or accounting accounts. Users enter
records manually or import supported files and approve changes. Imported categories, accounts, and
transactions use separate review-and-import checkpoints. Tersa does not initiate payments, transfers,
trades, tax filings, or credit applications.
The local encrypted database is Tersa's working source of truth. Individual CloudKit sync is off by default
and starts only when the user enables it. Household mode requires CloudKit sync after a specific
disclosure and consent step. In either mode, Tersa encrypts eligible financial records, attachment
contents, and attachment-key records before upload into the user's Apple iCloud environment. This is
application-level end-to-end encryption of that content, not a claim that the entire CloudKit architecture
or all related metadata is end-to-end encrypted. The Developer does not operate a separate Tersa
account or general-purpose financial-data server.
Tersa's deterministic financial engine calculates postings, balances, schedules, budgets, forecasts,
reports, and proposed budget amounts. The bundled local AI may propose structured drafts or explain
deterministic results, but it cannot autonomously commit, delete, export, or sync canonical financial
records. Every consequential change requires a user approval action. If the device-local Review before

5. Purposes and Privacy Principles
Tersa processes information to provide the features the user requests; secure and synchronize the
workspace; validate records; calculate reports; restore purchases; remember settings; prevent duplicate
or unsafe operations; maintain privacy-safe local diagnostics; investigate a user-reported problem when
the user chooses to share an export; enforce these Terms; and meet legal obligations.
The Developer intends to follow the Canadian fair-information principles of accountability, identifying
purposes, meaningful consent, limiting collection, limiting use, disclosure and retention, accuracy,
safeguards, openness, individual access, and the ability to challenge compliance. Tersa is designed to
minimize information sent outside the device.
Tersa does not use financial records, receipts, prompts, or AI proposals for advertising, data brokerage,
generalized model training, credit decisions, or profiling across unrelated services.


6. Local Storage and Encryption
Tersa stores the workspace in a SQLCipher-encrypted database in the app's protected container. The
database key is randomly generated and stored in the iOS Keychain with device-only, unlocked-device
protection. The database and attachments also use iOS file protection.
Attachments are encrypted separately with authenticated encryption. Tersa uses separate key lifecycles
for the local database, attachment files, CloudKit workspaces, attachment-key records, and portable
backups. Device key-agreement private keys and unwrapped workspace keys use device-only iOS
Keychain storage and are not synchronized through iCloud Keychain. This separation reduces risk but
does not make compromise impossible.
When this Policy describes synced financial content as end-to-end encrypted, it means Tersa is designed
to encrypt that content on a Tersa endpoint before CloudKit receives it and to decrypt it only on a Tersa
endpoint holding the required workspace key. Endpoints include approved Individual devices and key-
holding Household participant devices. The term does not mean that every CloudKit field or metadata
item is encrypted, that an endpoint or participant is trustworthy, that device or share membership is
independently proven by the ciphertext, or that the system is immune to compromised devices,
accounts, operating systems, software, approval flows, implementation defects, or active attacks.
If a required database, attachment, or workspace key is unavailable or ciphertext authentication fails,
Tersa is designed to fail closed rather than silently use plaintext or replace a different key. If every
approved device and every usable encrypted backup holding access to a workspace key is lost, synced
ciphertext may be permanently unrecoverable. Protecting the iPhone, passcode, Apple Account,
approved devices, and backup password remains the user's responsibility.

The bundled language model runs on the iPhone. Tersa does not configure it to call an external AI
service, browse the internet, read arbitrary files, query the full database, or mutate records directly. It
receives only context selected for the task and returns an untrusted proposal that is checked by
deterministic code.

8. Apple CloudKit and End-to-End Encryption of Individual Financial Content
Individual sync is off by default. While it remains off, Tersa does not prepare a CloudKit zone, discover an
existing route, fetch, or upload the workspace. If the user enables sync, Tersa uploads eligible data to a
private CloudKit database associated with the user's Apple Account. Apple provides account
authentication, storage, transport, and synchronization under Apple's terms and privacy policy.
Before upload, Tersa JSON-encodes and encrypts each eligible financial record with AES-256-GCM under
a random per-workspace key. The record name, kind, workspace identity, actor information, revision
lineage, and financial payload are authenticated inside the ciphertext. Attachment files are
independently sealed with AES-256-GCM, and each attachment content key is itself stored in a
workspace-encrypted record. This supports a content-level end-to-end encryption description because
CloudKit receives ciphertext rather than those plaintext financial values or an unwrapped workspace
key. It does not make the entire CloudKit record, service, or architecture end-to-end encrypted.
A new Individual device publishes a public key-agreement identity and displays a six-digit code derived
from the workspace and requesting public identity. An existing key-holding device must display the
matching code, its user must authenticate through iOS, and that device then wraps the workspace key
specifically for the requesting device. The code is a short human-comparison safeguard, not a secret,
password, digital signature, or independent cryptographic proof of device identity. A matching code and
device authentication reduce accidental or wrong-device approval risk but do not guarantee resistance
to every active key-substitution attempt, compromised Apple Account or platform, implementation
defect, or user mistake. The private key and unwrapped workspace key stay in device-only Keychain
storage. In normal operation, neither Apple nor the Developer receives that unwrapped key or holds a
master key designed to decrypt the financial content.

iCloud copy for later resumption, or request deletion of the iCloud copy while retaining local data under

9. Household Sharing and End-to-End Encryption of Financial Content
Household mode depends on Apple CloudKit sharing and encrypted iCloud sync. Before a Household
purchase, workspace creation, or invitation acceptance can lead to upload, Tersa presents that
requirement, explains the application-level encryption and key custody, and requires an affirmative
consent action. The sync control remains on while the Household workspace is active; a user who no
longer agrees must use the available leave, plan-transition, private-copy, backup, or deletion controls
rather than continue an active Household workspace without its required sync.
A household owner may create a shared CloudKit zone and invite participants. Tersa encrypts Household
financial records, attachment contents, and attachment-key records on a key-holding device before
CloudKit upload. A device identity published through an accepted CloudKit share may be treated as
eligible, and an online owner device may automatically create a device-specific wrapped workspace key
for that identity. The Household flow relies on Apple's share membership and access controls and does
not require the owner to compare a separate per-device code before every such wrap. In normal
operation, neither Apple nor the Developer receives an unwrapped workspace key or holds a master key
designed to decrypt the financial content.
End-to-end encryption applies to the protected financial content, not to the trustworthiness or separate
identity proof of every Household endpoint. It does not prevent invited or key-holding participants from
seeing the shared workspace. Participants may add, edit, reconcile, or delete shared records according
to the current role and subscription state. A participant may also view information on a compromised
device, capture screenshots, or export records outside Tersa.
Apple necessarily processes CloudKit account and share-participant information, zone and record
existence and timing, coarse payload class, routing and workspace-control values, pseudonymous
workspace, device, recipient, and attachment identifiers, public keys and wrapped-key envelopes,
attachment size descriptors including plaintext and ciphertext byte counts, change and deletion state,
and other service metadata. Apple stores the encrypted financial payloads and attachment ciphertext.
Tersa does not place plaintext financial values or plaintext workspace keys in control records, but it does
not control Apple's independent processing or claim that this metadata is end-to-end encrypted.
The owner should invite only trusted people, remove participants when access is no longer appropriate,
and understand that a removed participant may retain prior exports, screenshots, backups, or
information already learned.

10. Exchange Rates and Network Requests
Tersa permits network access only for narrowly defined services: Apple StoreKit and App Store
functions, Apple CloudKit and sharing, the configured exchange-rate providers, and user-initiated
external legal pages.
For an exchange-rate lookup, Tersa sends only the base currency, quote currency, and requested date
over HTTPS. Tersa tries Frankfurter at api.frankfurter.dev first and uses the open-source fawazahmed0
currency API through jsDelivr or Cloudflare Pages only for rates Frankfurter does not resolve. Requests
are designed without cookies or credentials; Frankfurter receives no financial request body, while
fallback currency codes and dates appear in the requested URL path. Providers and network
intermediaries may still receive ordinary connection data such as IP address, time, and device-network
information under their own practices.
Rates may be delayed, unavailable, rounded, revised, or inaccurate and are provided for record-keeping
estimates. A user should verify a rate against an authoritative source before making a payment, filing
tax, valuing an asset, or taking another consequential action.

authentication is optional and is performed by iOS; Tersa receives the result, not biometric material.
CloudKit permissions and account status are managed through Apple services.
Tersa can schedule local reminders. The default private summary hides descriptions and amounts. A

Tersa may obscure the app switcher and relock after a short background interval, but these features are

12. Imports, Exports, and Portable Backups
CSV, OFX, and QIF imports are parsed locally into an encrypted, resumable draft. Tersa reviews and
imports categories first, accounts second, and transactions last. Each accepted phase commits
independently; a completed category or account phase remains available if a later phase fails, is retried,
or is deliberately skipped. The user chooses Create new, Use existing, or Ignore for proposed catalog
objects and separately accepts the final transaction batch. Tersa may retain provenance needed to
explain or prevent duplicate imports. The source file remains under the user's control outside Tersa.
CSV, JSON, PDF, and diagnostic-log exports are not encrypted by Tersa after creation. The diagnostic log
is designed to exclude financial and personal content, but it still contains technical device and app
context. Once an export is saved to Files, Mail, Messages, AirDrop, a printer, another app, or another
person, it is governed by the user's choices and the receiving service's practices.
Portable .tersabackup files are encrypted with a user-chosen password using a memory-hard key
derivation function and authenticated encryption. The Developer does not know the password and

15. Retention, Deletion, and Workspace Control
Local workspace records remain until the user edits or deletes them, deletes the workspace, removes
the app, erases the device, or restores different data, subject to device and Apple backup behaviour.
Deleting an attachment does not necessarily delete the related financial transaction or history.
A user can clear learned AI preferences separately, export records, create an encrypted backup, and
request full workspace deletion through Tersa. Before execution, Tersa displays a separate final warning
with a prominent alert icon, states that deletion cannot be undone, recommends creating an encrypted
backup if recovery might be wanted, and requires device authentication. Deleting a workspace does not
reset an already-used trial and does not delete exported files, screenshots, printed copies, recipient
copies, or backups stored elsewhere.
The universal diagnostic log is separate from the financial workspace, excluded from backup and sync,
and can be cleared by the user. Tersa prunes an entry when it becomes older than five days and also
prunes the oldest entries whenever the log exceeds 5 MB. A Siri Quick Fill draft is deleted after one
consumption attempt and is invalid after ten minutes.
