 3. How ClearScribe Works
 ClearScribe offers three ways to provide audio for transcription: recording directly from the device
 microphone, importing an audio file from the Files app or other sources, or selecting a video from Apple
 Photos to extract its audio track.
 Audio is decoded to 16 kHz mono PCM format and processed in sequential segments by the bundled
 on-device speech recognition model. Transcripts are generated locally with optional timestamps and can
 be exported as plain text, subtitles (SRT), or PDF documents.

 ClearScribe stores recordings and transcripts in the app’s private sandbox. The app never modifies,
 moves, or deletes original files from Apple Photos, iCloud Drive, or the Files app.

 5. Purposes of Local Processing
 ClearScribe processes permitted information only to provide app functionality: record audio, decode audio
 from imported files or video, transcribe speech using the local AI model, generate timestamps, store
 transcripts, export in multiple formats, and manage model downloads and user preferences.

 The app may create local technical records to track transcription progress, model state, and trial usage.
 Those records are not uploaded, used for advertising, or used to identify a person across apps, devices,
 or services.

 6. Permissions, Consent, and User Choices
 ClearScribe requests microphone access to record audio. It requests file access or Apple Photos access
 to import audio and video files. The user may grant or deny each permission independently, subject to the
 options iOS provides.

 Permissions can be changed or revoked through iPhone Settings at any time. Revoking microphone
 access prevents live recording. Revoking file or photo access prevents importing. Revoking permissions
 does not delete recordings or transcripts already stored in ClearScribe.
 ClearScribe never modifies or deletes original files from Apple Photos, iCloud Drive, or the Files app.
 Recordings and transcripts stored within ClearScribe can be deleted by the user at any time from within
 the app.

 7. Local AI Processing
 The bundled Whisper speech recognition model and supporting libraries run on the iPhone. ClearScribe
 does not configure the model to call an external AI service or upload audio content, prompts, or
 generated transcripts.
 AI output is probabilistic. Transcriptions may contain inaccuracies, errors, omissions, misinterpretations,
 or fabrications. ClearScribe may incorrectly transcribe names, numbers, technical terms, foreign words,
 or other content. ClearScribe is not a verbatim court reporter, medical transcription service, accessibility
 substitute, or professional record-management system.

 Every transcript is presented for user review. The user is responsible for verifying and editing transcripts
 before relying on, exporting, or sharing them.

 8. Local Storage, Retention, and Deletion
 ClearScribe stores preferences, recordings, transcript history, and model files in the app’s private
 container. These records generally remain until the user deletes individual transcripts, removes the app,
 or erases the device.

 Temporary working files, including decoded PCM audio samples, are designed to be removed after use.
 Unexpected termination, operating-system behavior, backups, or storage failure may delay or prevent
 immediate removal.
 Deleting ClearScribe normally removes its private app container from the device, subject to iOS and
 backup behavior. It does not delete original files from Apple Photos, iCloud Drive, or the Files app.

 Device backups and iCloud backups, if enabled, are controlled by Apple and the user’s settings. Restored
 backups may restore local app records.

 ClearScribe includes third-party software and model components that execute locally. Their inclusion
 does not authorize their authors to access the user’s recordings through ClearScribe. Apple and the
